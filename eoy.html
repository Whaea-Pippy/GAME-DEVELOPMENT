Kēmu Kāri — Kōrero Māori anō!
Card game — speak Māori while you play

Tākaro kāri, kōrero i te reo Māori, ā, hopukina he ataata poto o tō rōpū e mahi ana.

Ngā Rerenga Kōrero (Te Reo Māori)

Whakamahia ēnei rerenga i a koutou e tākaro ana. Kōrero Māori anake!

  • Pīrangi tākaro kāri? — Wanna play cards?
  • Ko au te toa! — I'm the winner!
  • Kua hinga koe! — You lose!
  • Tētahi tāhae! — Cheater!
  • E kore ā muri e hokia! — What's done is done!
  • Kei a koe! — Your turn!
  • Huraina ngā kāri! — Shuffle the cards!
  • Tohaina ngā kāri. — Deal the cards.
  • He kīngi āu? — Got any kings?
  • He rauna anō? — Another round?

Instructions — English

Use the phrases on the left when you play cards. Record a short video of your group speaking Māori while you play. Share the video with your teacher.

Before you start / Ī mua i te tīmatanga
  1. Make groups of 3–5 students. / Waihangatia he rōpū 3–5 tāngata.
  2. Choose a card game (e.g. Memory, Go Fish, Snap). / Kōwhiria tētahi kēmu kāri.
  3. Set up one device to record. / Whakaritea he pūrere hei hopu ataata.
While you play / I te wā e tākaro ana
  • Speak only Māori — try to use at least 8 phrases. / Kōrero Māori anake, whakamahia kia 8 rerenga neke atu.
  • One person counts the phrases. / Kotahi te tangata hei tatau i ngā rerenga.
  • Be loud and proud! / Kōrero māia, kōrero kaha!
